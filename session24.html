01. VENDOR ONBOARDING

Risk starts before the contract is signed.

Due Diligence & Assessment

Evaluating the Partner

Researching a vendor’s security posture before granting access to data or systems.

  • Reviewing SOC 2 Type II reports.
  • Security questionnaires (SIG/CAIQ).
  • Background checks on key personnel.

"Trust but verify: Never assume a big name equals big security."

Agreements Part 1: SLA & MOU

SLA

Service Level Agreement. Specifies performance requirements. (e.g., "99.9% uptime"). Features penalties for failure.

MOU

Memorandum of Understanding. A non-binding agreement that defines a common goal or partnership between two parties.

Agreements Part 2: MOA & ISA

MOA

Memorandum of Agreement. Similar to an MOU but often more specific about duties and responsibilities. Can be legally binding.

ISA

Interconnection Security Agreement. Specifically defines the technical security requirements for two organizations connecting their networks.

02. SUPPLY CHAIN RISK

Protecting the roots of your technology.

The Hidden Threat

Software Supply Chain

Compromising legitimate software before it reaches the customer. (e.g., SolarWinds).

  • Malicious code in library dependencies.
  • Hardware backdoors planted during manufacturing.

"If you didn't build it, you're trusting whoever did."

Right to Audit Clauses

The Verification Clause

A contractual provision that allows the customer to review the vendor's security books, data, and processes.

  • Ensures the vendor is actually doing what they promised.
  • Vital for compliance in regulated industries (Finance, Health).

Secure Termination

The Exit Strategy

What happens when the relationship ends? Secure offboarding is just as critical as onboarding.

  • Revoking all logical and physical access.
  • Data return or verified destruction.
  • NDAs and non-compete enforcement.
