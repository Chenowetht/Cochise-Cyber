Session 21: Domain 5.1 — The Security Program Blueprint. Exploring the policies, regulations, and industry standards that shape enterprise security.

SY0-701 PREP

01. GOVERNANCE

Ensuring security supports business goals.

The Hierarchy of Intent

Policy

High-level statement of intent. Mandatory. (e.g., "All passwords must be strong").

Standard

Specific mandatory requirements. (e.g., "Passwords must be 14 characters").

Procedure

Step-by-step instructions. (e.g., "How to reset your password").

Guideline

Recommended actions. Not mandatory. (e.g., "Suggested password managers").

Types of Frameworks

Regulatory

Required by law. (HIPAA, GDPR). Failure leads to legal action.

Non-Regulatory

Best practices not required by law. (NIST CSF). High quality, but voluntary.

Industry-Specific

Required by industry standards. (PCI DSS for credit cards).

"Frameworks provide a common language for security and audit."

NIST Cybersecurity Framework

Identify

Inventory assets and risks.

Protect

Implement safeguards.

Detect

Monitor for anomalies.

Respond

Take action on incidents.

Recover

Restore resilience.

02. COMPLIANCE & REGS

The legal boundary of security.

Privacy & Data Laws

  • GDPR: European Union privacy law. Grants "Right to be Forgotten."
  • HIPAA: US law protecting healthcare data (PHI).
  • PCI DSS: Global standard for entities handling credit cards.
  • SOX: US law requiring security for financial reporting.

ISO/IEC 27001 & 27002

ISO 27001

The Requirement. Defines how to build an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

ISO 27002

The Code of Practice. Detailed list of security controls used to satisfy 27001.

Hardening Benchmarks

CIS Benchmarks

Center for Internet Security. Provides the industry "Gold Standard" for hardening specific OS, servers, and cloud apps.

"Use CIS to move from a default install to a secure install."
