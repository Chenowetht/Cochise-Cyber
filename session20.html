01. DEFINING RISK

Risk = Probability × Impact

The Three Elements

Asset

Anything of value to the organization. (Data, hardware, people, reputation).

Threat

An external or internal force that could cause harm. (Malware, hurricane, disgruntled employee).

Vulnerability

A weakness that a threat can exploit. (Unpatched OS, weak password, lack of training).

Risk Analysis Methods

Qualitative

Based on subjective judgment and experience. Uses scales like "Low, Medium, High" or 1-10 scores.

Quantitative

Based on objective data and dollar values. Uses mathematical formulas to predict financial loss.

"Most organizations use a blend of both for comprehensive visibility."

The Math of Risk

SLE (Single Loss Expectancy) = Asset Value × Exposure Factor

ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy) = SLE × ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence)

Example: If a $1,000 laptop is stolen (SLE) twice a year (ARO), the ALE is $2,000.

Appetite vs. Tolerance

  • Risk Appetite: The broad amount of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its goals.
  • Risk Tolerance: The specific degree of variance from the appetite that is acceptable for a particular project.
  • Inherent Risk: Risk level before any controls are implemented.
  • Residual Risk: Risk that remains after controls are implemented.

02. RISK STRATEGIES

Choosing how to handle identified risks.

The Response Matrix

Mitigation

Applying a control to reduce risk.
Example: Installing a firewall.

Transference

Moving the risk to a 3rd party.
Example: Buying cyber insurance.

Acceptance

Doing nothing because the cost of control > cost of loss.

Avoidance

Stopping the activity that creates the risk.
Example: Disabling public Wi-Fi.

The Risk Register

The Master Document

A central database that tracks all identified risks, their severity, their owners, and the status of remediation efforts.

"If it's not in the register, it's not being managed."
