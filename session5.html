01. DELIVERY MECHANISMS

How software spreads and infects hosts.

Viruses vs. Worms

Viruses (Host-Dependent)

Code that attaches to a legitimate program and requires human interaction to execute and spread.

Worms (Self-Replicating)

Stand-alone programs that spread across networks automatically by exploiting vulnerabilities. No human interaction required.

"A virus is a hitchhiker; a worm is its own vehicle."

Trojans & RATs

"Beware of gifts that appear useful."

Malicious Disguise

Programs that appear useful but hide a malicious payload.

  • RAT (Remote Access Trojan): Provides the attacker with full control over the infected host.
  • Goal: Establish a backdoor for persistent access.

Ransomware & Cryptomalware

The Extortion Model

Inhibits the use of a computer or data until a ransom is paid.

  • Cryptomalware: Uses strong encryption to lock files.
  • Extortion: Threatens to leak sensitive data (Double Extortion).

"The primary defense is an offline, air-gapped backup strategy."

02. STEALTH & PERSISTENCE

Hiding from the OS and security tools.

Rootkits & Fileless Malware

Rootkits

Modifies the OS kernel to hide its presence. Detectable via boot-time scans or behavioral analysis.

Fileless Malware

Operates entirely in RAM and uses legitimate system tools (like PowerShell) to evade disk-based detection.

Spyware & Keyloggers

Spyware

Secretly monitors user activity, browsing history, and data without consent.

Keyloggers

Records keystrokes to steal passwords, credit card numbers, and PII.

Logic Bombs & Botnets

  • Logic Bomb: Malicious code that waits for a specific trigger (date, time, or action) to execute.
  • Botnet: A network of infected "zombie" computers controlled by a Command & Control (C2) server.
  • DDoS: Botnets are the primary tool for massive Distributed Denial of Service attacks.
