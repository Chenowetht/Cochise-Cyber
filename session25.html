GOVERNANCE MASTERY

Domain 5 Overview

Risk & Compliance

Calculating loss (ALE), choosing risk strategies, and adhering to legal/regulatory frameworks (GDPR, HIPAA).

Governance & Vendors

Structuring policies, verifying via audits (SOC 2), and managing external supply chain risks (SLAs).

Quantitative Risk Math

ALE = SLE × ARO

SLE (Asset Value × Exposure Factor) is the cost of ONE event.

ARO is how many times it happens in a year.

Example: If a $100,000 server has a 50% EF, the SLE is $50,000. If ARO is 0.1 (once every 10 years), the ALE is $5,000.

The Four Risk Strategies

Mitigate

Reduce risk with a control. (e.g., Installing an IPS).

Transfer

Shift the burden. (e.g., Insurance or Outsourcing).

Accept

Do nothing. Cost of control > Cost of loss.

Avoid

Stop the risky activity. (e.g., Turning off public WiFi).

The Hierarchy of Documentation

  • 1. Policy: High-level management intent. Mandatory.
  • 2. Standard: Specific requirements. Mandatory.
  • 3. Procedure: Step-by-step instructions. Mandatory.
  • 4. Guideline: Recommendations. Optional/Flexible.

Data Privacy & States

PII vs. PHI

PII: Identity trackers (SSN, Email).
PHI: Health records (HIPAA compliance).

States of Data

At Rest: Storage (AES).
In Transit: Network (TLS).
In Use: RAM (Enclaves).

Verification & SOC Reporting

SOC 2 Reports

Type I: Evaluates design at a specific point in time.

Type II: Evaluates effectiveness over a period (e.g., 6 months).

"Auditor Independence is non-negotiable for credibility."

Vendor & Supply Chain

SLA

Service Level. Performance and uptime requirements.

MOU / MOA

Agreements on goals and specific responsibilities.

ISA

Interconnection. Technical security for connecting nets.
