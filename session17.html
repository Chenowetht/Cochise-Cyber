01. IDENTIFICATION

The continuous cycle of discovery and assessment.

Scanning Strategies

Non-Credentialed

Outside-in view. What an attacker sees. Shows open ports and missing patches visible to the network.

Credentialed

Inside-out view. High accuracy. Scans local files, registry keys, and account configurations. Lower impact on network.

Scanner Accuracy

False Positive

"The alarm went off, but there is no fire."

Scanner reports a vulnerability that does not actually exist or isn't applicable.

False Negative

"The house is burning, but the alarm is silent."

The most dangerous scenario. A vulnerability exists, but the scanner missed it.

02. PRIORITIZATION

Using CVSS to decide what to fix first.

CVSS 3.1 Framework

Base Score

Intrinsic qualities. Does it require physical access? Is it easy to exploit? Does it break CIA?

Temporal Score

Changes over time. Is there an exploit kit available? Is there a patch ready?

Environmental

Context-specific. Is this server in a DMZ or air-gapped? How critical is this specific host?

Score Range: 0.0 (None) to 10.0 (Critical).

Pentesting vs. Scanning

Vulnerability Scan

Automated, passive identification. Finds "potential" risks. Cheap and frequent.

Penetration Test

Manual, active exploitation. Proves "actual" risks by bypassing controls. Expensive and periodic.

"A scanner tells you the door is unlocked; a pentester walks through it and steals the silver."

The Colored Teams

  • Red Team: The attackers. Attempting to bypass security and remain undetected.
  • Blue Team: The defenders. Monitoring logs and responding to the red team.
  • Purple Team: Collaborative. Red and Blue work together to improve overall security.
  • White Team: The referees. Setting the rules and managing the engagement.

Remediation Workflows

Patching

Updating the software to the latest version (Primary choice).

Compensating

Adding a firewall rule or WAF signature if no patch exists.

Acceptance

Acknowledging the risk but doing nothing because the cost outweighs the impact.
