01. THREAT ACTORS

Defining the entity responsible for an incident.

Nation States & APTs

Advanced Persistent Threats

Highly sophisticated, well-funded organizations often sponsored by governments.

  • Motivation: Strategic interest, espionage, or theft of intellectual property.
  • Persistence: They aim to remain undetected for long periods.
  • Capability: High-end custom tools and zero-day exploits.

"The most dangerous adversary due to infinite resources and long-term patience."

Insider Threats

"Trust is a vulnerability."

Types of Insiders

Anyone with authorized access who poses a risk.

  • Malicious: Disgruntled employees seeking revenge or profit.
  • Unintentional: Employees who make mistakes or fall for phishing.
  • Shadow IT: Users bypassing security for "productivity."

Script Kiddies & Hacktivists

Script Kiddies

Unskilled individuals who use existing scripts and tools. Motivation: Thrills or bragging rights.

Hacktivists

Attackers motivated by a social, religious, or political cause. Motivation: Ideology.

02. ATTACK VECTORS

The path an attacker takes to compromise a target.

Primary Attack Vectors

Email

Phishing, malicious attachments, and social engineering links.

Wireless

Rogue APs, evil twins, and protocol vulnerabilities (WPA2/3).

Supply Chain

Compromising vendors or third-party software libraries.

Digital vs. Physical Vectors

Web & Cloud

Exploiting misconfigured S3 buckets or web application vulnerabilities (SQLi, XSS).

Physical Access

Tailgating, USB drops, or theft of unprotected hardware.

Adversary Attributes

Attribute Description
Capability Technical skill level and custom tool availability.
Intent The ultimate goal (Espionage, Profit, Disruption).
Funding Financial resources available for the attack.
Opportunity Access to specific vulnerabilities or timing.
