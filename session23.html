01. THE AUDIT MISSION

Testing reality against policy.

Who is watching?

Internal Audit

Performed by employees of the organization. Focuses on internal process improvement and preparation for external reviews.

External Audit

Performed by an independent third-party firm. Provides formal attestation of compliance to shareholders, regulators, or customers.

"Internal identifies the gaps; External certifies the bridge."

Auditor Independence

Critical Separation

Auditors must remain independent from the team that manages the systems being audited to prevent "Conflict of Interest."

  • No shared management.
  • No "marking your own homework."
  • Focus on objective evidence.

"Without independence, the audit report has no legal or market value."

02. ATTESTATION & REPORTING

Documenting the results for stakeholders.

SOC 2 Framework

Type I (Point-in-Time)

Tests if the security controls are *designed* correctly as of a specific date.

Type II (Historical)

Tests if the controls were *operating effectively* over a period of time (e.g., 6 months).

"Type II is the gold standard for cloud service providers."

Attestation & Verification

Self-Assessment

Internal review where teams check their own work. Low reliability for outsiders.

Attestation

A third party provides a formal opinion that the claims made by management are true.

Certification

Confirmation that a system meets a specific set of rigorous standards (e.g., ISO 27001).

Evidence Collection

  • Audit Logs: Immutable records of who accessed what and when.
  • Observation: Watching a guard perform a patrol or an admin perform a backup.
  • Inquiry: Interviewing employees to verify they understand security policy.
  • Inspection: Reviewing actual configurations, patch logs, and firewall rules.

Continuous vs. Periodic

Periodic (Traditional)

The "Annual Audit." Snapshots that can miss threats between reviews.

Continuous Monitoring

Using automated tools to verify compliance in real-time. (e.g., Cloud Config alerts).

"In the cloud, an annual audit is already obsolete the day it is finished."
