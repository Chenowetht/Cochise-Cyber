01. EXAM DAY LOGISTICS

What happens at the Pearson VUE center.

Arrival & Identification

Check-In Process

  • IDs: Two forms of original, valid (unexpired) IDs. One must be government-issued with a photo.
  • Arrival: Arrive 15-30 minutes early. Pearson VUE is strict with timing.
  • Personal Items: Everything goes in a locker (phones, watches, hats, keys).

"The room will be quiet, proctored, and monitored by video."

Mastering the Interface

Flagging

Use the "Mark for Review" button. Do not get stuck on a question. Flag it and keep moving.

Elimination

Right-click options to strike them out (visual aid). Narrow it down to 50/50 before guessing.

Back Button

You can change any answer at any point until you click the final "Submit" button.

02. EXAM STRATEGY

The tactical approach to a 90-minute gauntlet.

The "Two-Pass" Method

Pass 1 (Pacing)

Skip the PBQs. Answer every multiple-choice question you feel 80% confident in. Flag the rest.

Pass 2 (Grind)

Go back to the PBQs. Use the momentum from Pass 1 to tackle the simulations and the hard-flagged questions.

"Never leave a question blank. There is no penalty for guessing."

Reading CompTIA Code

Watch for the Keywords:

  • "MOST likely" / "LEAST effective" (Comparing two good options).
  • "FIRST step" (Looking for the beginning of a lifecycle, not the end).
  • "BEST mitigation" (Looking for the technical control vs the administrative policy).

The Mental Game

  • Breathe: If you hit 3 hard questions in a row, close your eyes for 5 seconds.
  • Don't Overthink: Your first instinct is usually correct. Only change an answer if you found new evidence in another question.
  • Time Check: Aim for 1 minute per multiple-choice question.

The Aftermath

Instant Results

You will receive your score immediately after finishing the post-exam survey.

CEUs

The certification is valid for 3 years. You maintain it through Continuing Education Units (CEUs).

"Passing score: 750 / 900."
