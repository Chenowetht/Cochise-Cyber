01. THE HUMAN FIREWALL

Why attackers target people instead of software.

Phishing Variations

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks aimed at a specific individual or organization.

Whaling

Targeting high-level executives (CEOs, CFOs) for high-value data/money.

Vishing

Phishing via voice calls or automated voice recordings.

Smishing & Pharming

Smishing

Phishing via SMS text messages. Often includes a link or a sense of urgency about a bank account.

Pharming

Redirecting users to a fake website even if they type the correct URL. Achieved via DNS poisoning or host file modification.

Physical Manipulation

  • Tailgating: Following an authorized person through a door without their knowledge.
  • Piggybacking: The authorized person knowingly allows someone to follow them (e.g., holding the door).
  • Shoulder Surfing: Watching a user enter a PIN, password, or sensitive data.
  • Dumpster Diving: Searching through trash for sensitive documents or technical manuals.

02. PRINCIPLES OF INFLUENCE

The psychological triggers used to bypass logic.

Authority & Intimidation

Authority

Posing as a CEO, IT manager, or police officer. People are conditioned to obey orders from high-ranking figures.

Intimidation

Using fear or threats (e.g., "Your account will be deleted permanently") to force a quick, unthinking response.

Urgency & Scarcity

Urgency

Creating a time-limited scenario to prevent the victim from performing research or thinking critically.

Scarcity

Suggesting a resource is in limited supply (e.g., "Only 3 gift cards left!") to trigger impulsive behavior.

Consensus & Familiarity

  • Consensus (Social Proof): Convincing the victim that others have already done the action (e.g., "Everyone else on your team updated their password").
  • Familiarity/Liking: Building a rapport or using a mutual connection to lower the victim's defenses.
  • Trust: Establishing a believable pretext or relationship over time.
