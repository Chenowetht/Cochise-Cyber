01. CONTROL CATEGORIES

Classifying controls by WHO or WHAT implements them.

Technical (Logical)

Implementation: Hardware & Software.

Security measures automated by information systems.

  • Firewalls & IPS: Traffic filtering.
  • Encryption: Data protection.
  • MFA/Biometrics: Identification systems.

"If it requires a CPU to function, it's likely a Technical Control."

Managerial (Administrative)

"Focuses on the administrative side of security management."

Implementation: Policy & Planning.

Strategic oversight and regulatory compliance measures.

  • Risk Assessments: Identifying threats.
  • Written Policies: Acceptable Use, Passwords.
  • Background Checks: Hiring processes.

Operational

Implementation: People & Processes.

Day-to-day procedures performed by humans to maintain security.

  • Security Training: Raising user awareness.
  • Physical Security: Guards and patrols.
  • Configuration Mgmt: Consistent builds.

"If it's a routine human activity, it's an Operational Control."

02. CONTROL FUNCTIONS

Classifying controls by WHEN they act in an incident.

Functional Classifications

Preventive

Stops incidents before they occur. (Firewalls, Guards, Encryption)

Detective

Identifies incidents while/after they occur. (IDS, Log audits, Cameras)

Corrective

Fixes damage or restores systems. (Backups, Patching, IR plans)

Deterrent & Physical

Deterrent

Discourages a potential attacker.
Example: "Warning" signs, fake cameras.

Physical

Measures that touch the real world.
Example: Fences, locks, mantraps.

Compensating

The "Plan B"

An alternative control used when a primary control is too expensive, technically unfeasible, or impossible to implement.

"If you can't use MFA because a legacy system doesn't support it, requiring 20-character passwords plus hardware isolation acts as a Compensating Control."
