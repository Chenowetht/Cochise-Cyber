D1 Recap: Security Pillars

Core Concepts

The CIA Triad remains the yardstick for every security decision:

  • Confidentiality: Encryption, Access Controls.
  • Integrity: Hashing, Digital Signatures.
  • Availability: Redundancy, Backups, Patching.

D1 Recap: Control Framework

Technical

Logical software/hardware solutions (e.g., Firewall rules).

Managerial

Administrative oversight and policy (e.g., Risk assessments).

Operational

Human processes and training (e.g., Guard patrols).

D2 Recap: The Adversary

Common Threat Actors

Understand the Motivation and Funding of your adversary:

  • Nation State: Strategic interest, APTs.
  • Hacktivist: Ideological or political cause.
  • Insider: Trust, disgruntled or accidental.
  • Script Kiddie: Low skill, thrill-seeking.

D2 Recap: Malware Families

Persistence

RATs, Rootkits, and Backdoors aim to stay long-term.

Impact

Ransomware, Botnets, and Logic Bombs cause disruption.

Stealth

Fileless malware and Spyware avoid detection mechanisms.

D2 Recap: Social Engineering

Psychological Triggers

Attackers exploit: Authority, Urgency, Scarcity, Consensus, and Trust.

Whether it is Whaling (targeting C-suite) or Vishing (voice phishing), the goal is to bypass logic through emotion.

Connecting the Concepts

The Attack Flow

A Threat Actor uses a specific Vector to deliver Malware, breaking Integrity or Confidentiality.

The Defense Flow

We implement Technical Controls to detect the breach and Operational Controls to recover via backups.
