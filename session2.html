01. THE CIA TRIAD

The three critical objectives of any security program.

Confidentiality

Goal: Keep secrets secret.

Ensuring that data is not disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes.

  • Encryption: Scrambling data at rest or in transit.
  • Access Control: Permissions and ACLs.
  • Obfuscation: Making code/data difficult to read.

"If the wrong person sees it, Confidentiality is broken."

Integrity

"If the data was changed without permission, Integrity is broken."

Goal: Keep data accurate.

Ensuring that data has not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner.

  • Hashing: Creating a digital "fingerprint" of data.
  • Digital Signatures: Proving source and integrity.
  • Version Control: Tracking changes over time.

Availability

Goal: Keep systems running.

Ensuring that systems and data are accessible and usable when required by authorized users.

  • Redundancy: Load balancers and failover clusters.
  • Backups: Recovery from data loss or ransomware.
  • Patching: Closing vulnerabilities that cause crashes.

"If you can't get to the data when you need it, Availability is broken."

02. THE AAA FRAMEWORK

The backbone of Access Management.

Authentication, Authorization, Accounting

Authentication

"Are you who you say you are?" (Passwords, MFA, Biometrics)

Authorization

"What are you allowed to do?" (Permissions, Roles, Scopes)

Accounting

"What did you do?" (Logs, Audit trails, Tracking)

Non-Repudiation

The "No Denying" Rule

The assurance that someone cannot deny the validity of something. Non-repudiation is a legal concept as much as a technical one.

  • Digital signatures link a user to a specific action.
  • Tamper-proof logs provide evidence of events.

Zero Trust Architecture

"Never Trust, Always Verify"

Traditional security relied on a "hard perimeter" (Castle and Moat). Zero Trust assumes the breach has already happened.

  • Continuous Verification: Every request is authenticated.
  • Least Privilege: Only what is needed, only when needed.
  • Microsegmentation: Dividing the network into tiny zones.
