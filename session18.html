01. NETWORK VISIBILITY

You cannot protect what you cannot see.

Flow Analysis (NetFlow / IPFIX)

Metadata, not Packets

Flow monitoring records the "Who, When, and How" of network traffic without capturing the actual content (payload).

  • Source/Destination IPs & Ports.
  • Timestamp and duration of the session.
  • Volume of data transferred.

"Like looking at a phone bill: you see who called whom, but not what they said."

Protocol Analyzers (Sniffers)

Full Packet Capture

Capturing and decoding the actual payload of every packet.
Examples: Wireshark, tcpdump.

Deep Dive Analysis

Used for troubleshooting complex issues or forensic investigation of cleartext traffic.

  • Viewing HTTP headers, DNS queries, or unencrypted passwords.
  • Analyzing malformed packets or scanning attempts.

SNMP Mechanics

Poll (Request)

Manager asks Agent: "How much CPU are you using?"

Trap (Alert)

Agent tells Manager: "My power supply just failed! (Unsolicited notification)."

SNMPv3

The only secure version. Adds Authentication and Encryption.

02. ANALYSIS & BASELINING

Turning data into actionable intelligence.

What is a Baseline?

Defining "Normal"

A snapshot of network/system performance over a specific period. Without a baseline, you cannot identify an anomaly.

  • Average bandwidth usage on a Tuesday morning.
  • Typical login times for employees.

"Anomaly detection is simply the measurement of deviation from the baseline."

Performance Metrics

Throughput

The actual rate of successful data delivery over a channel.

Latency

The delay between the request and the response. (Lag).

Jitter

Variation in latency. Critical for VoIP and Video traffic.

Interface Monitoring

  • Up/Down Status: Is the port active?
  • Error Rates: Are packets being dropped due to bad cables or interference?
  • Utilization: Is the link saturated (bottleneck)?
  • Environmentals: Rack temperature and humidity levels.
