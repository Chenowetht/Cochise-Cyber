01. HIGH AVAILABILITY

Building systems that don't go down.

Hardware Redundancy

Eliminating Single Points of Failure

Duplicating critical hardware to ensure continuous operation.

  • Dual PSUs: Redundant power supplies connected to different circuits.
  • NIC Teaming: Using multiple network cards for failover/throughput.
  • Clustering: Linking servers to act as a single unit (Active-Active/Passive).

"Redundancy is the path to Reliability."

Storage Resilience: RAID

RAID 0

Striping. Performance only. No redundancy. (Not fault tolerant).

RAID 1

Mirroring. Exact copy of data. High redundancy.

RAID 5

Striping with Parity. Can lose 1 drive. Efficient storage.

RAID 6/10

RAID 6: Lose 2 drives. RAID 10: Mirroring + Striping.

Power Protection

UPS

Uninterruptible Power Supply. Instant battery backup for short-term surges or outages.

Generators

Long-term power solution during extended utility failures.

"PDU (Power Distribution Unit) manages power flow within the rack."

02. DISASTER RECOVERY

What happens when the building is gone?

Site Types

  • Hot Site: Fully functional, real-time data sync. Recovery in minutes. Most expensive.
  • Warm Site: Hardware is there, but data must be restored. Recovery in hours/days.
  • Cold Site: Empty room with power/cooling. Hardware and data must be shipped. Recovery in weeks.

Backups: The 3-2-1 Rule

The Golden Rule

3 copies of data.
2 different media types.
1 offsite copy (Air-gapped).

Backup Types

  • Full: Everything. Slowest to back up, fastest to restore.
  • Incremental: Changes since last full or incremental. Fastest back up.
  • Differential: Changes since last full.

Recovery Metrics

RTO

Recovery Time Objective: Maximum acceptable downtime before business is impacted.

RPO

Recovery Point Objective: Maximum acceptable data loss (measured in time, e.g., "last 4 hours").

"These define your backup frequency and site type requirements."
