01. NETWORK ZONES

Defining trust boundaries and containment.

Segmentation & VLANs

Breaking Up the Network

Dividing a network into smaller, isolated parts to improve performance and security.

  • VLANs: Logical segmentation at Layer 2.
  • Subnetting: IP-based segmentation at Layer 3.
  • Goal: Containment (Blast Radius reduction).

"Prevents lateral movement during a breach."

The DMZ / Screened Subnet

Public-Facing

Web, Mail, and DNS servers live here.

The Buffer Zone

A semi-trusted area between the public internet and the private internal network.

  • Uses two firewalls or a 3-legged firewall.
  • Protects internal assets from external exposure.

Air-Gapping

Total Physical Isolation

A network that is physically disconnected from any other network, especially the internet.

  • Highest level of security.
  • Common in SCADA/ICS and high-security gov labs.

"If it's not connected, it can't be remotely hacked."

02. TRAFFIC FLOWS

Analyzing the direction of communication.

Analyzing Directions

East-West

Traffic moving laterally between systems inside the data center/network.

North-South

Traffic entering or leaving the network (Client to Server).

"Modern attacks focus on lateral movement (East-West)."

Firewalls & Proxies

Types of Firewalls

  • Stateless: Fast, but only looks at individual packets (ACLs).
  • Stateful: Tracks the context of the connection.
  • NGFW (Next-Gen): Application-aware (Layer 7).

Proxies

  • Forward Proxy: Protects internal clients by hiding them from servers.
  • Reverse Proxy: Protects servers by hiding them from internet clients.

Load Balancers

Distributes traffic to avoid server overload.

High Availability Tools

Ensuring Availability through intelligent routing.

  • Layer 4: Routes by IP/Port.
  • Layer 7: Routes by Content (URL, Cookies).
  • Health Checks: Stops routing to failed nodes.
